Original Travels

Frederiksberg Gardens

Frederiksberg, Denmark

Frederiksberg Gardens

Frederiksberg Gardens is set near Frederiksberg Palace during the reign of King Frederik IV until 1730. This hidden gem was beside our Airbnb and can be accessible by public. Most people came out in the evenings to either run or to eat supper. We didnt have enough time to check out the Palace but there is a Chinese Pavillion near by that was built in 1799. What I absolutely love about this place is the symmetrical alignment all around the garden and the preserved presence of royalty.

Assistens Cemetary

Nørrebro, Denmark

Assistens Cemetary

Walking around a cemetary doesn't sound like a norm, however the Danes treat this as a "walk in the park". This is Denmark's best known cemetary covering over 25 hectares of land. This is also the burial place of Hans Christian Andersen (a famous fairy tale author), who wrote "The Little Mermaid", "The Ugly Duckling", etc. Surprisingly, we spent a lot of time walking and deciphering tombstones. Truly a place where the living are among the dead. Another thing that amused me is both notable Danes and homeless people are buried in the same place. When I further looked into it, Danes (Nordics & Scandanavians) follow the Law of Jante, one of which states: "You shall not imagine that you are any better than us.” HUMBLE.

Old Town

Gamla Stan, Sweden

Old Town Gamla Stan

Old Town Stockholm has hidden remains of Sweds livelihood back when candles were the only source of light and building structures where held up with cast iron signs starting from the 16th century. Prepare for the cobble stone roads, join the walking tour and walk through the Mårten Trotzigs Gränd (narrowest alley).

Lake Mälaren

Gröndal, Sweden

Lake Mälaren in Gröndal, Sweden

This breathtaking view was taken when my brother and I ventured after dropping our luggage off at our Airbnb. Off the roads of Fregattvägen and up the rocks, this place was purely a gem away from touristy Stockholm. If you make it safely down the rocks and weather permits, you can swim in the waters and pet a few dogs.

Drottningholm Palace

Drottningholm, Sweden

Drottningholm Palace

Where the Swedish royal King & Queen reside also accessible by public, this palace is a subway and bus ride away. You can still see guards who corner every inch around the palace. Behind the palace are gardens, palace theatre and church all accessible by the public built in the 18th century.

Uspenski Cathedral

Helsinki, Finland

Uspenski Cathedral

We decided to roam around in wait for our 8 hour bus to Oulu and found this beauty in central Helsinki. You can walk behind the church which faces the waters of the South Harbour. I am still amazed at the sheer number of churches, were all believers and why didn't they go to the same church? 21st century and I still question it.

Temppeliaukio Church

Töölö, Finland

Temppeliaukio Church (known as Rock Church)

Rock church carved from solid rock back in 1969. The first time I came here I was lost, I got behind the rock church and couldn't find the entrance. It seemed to me like a BIG ROCK. Without losing hope, I came a second time with a friend who had been here before. Entry costs 3€, but has alluring interior rugged walls along with natural mineral acoustics. Church and concert hall... 'nuff said.

Helsinki Cathedral @ Senate Square

Helsinki, Finland

Helsinki Cathedral at Senate Square

Located at Senate Square with the statue of Alexander II, dates back to the Finnish autonomy from Russia. In this square is also the Helsinki Cathedral, University of Helsinki and the Government Palace. As for the picture, I just had to 😃

"The Times We Had." Wall

Toompea Hill, Estonia

"The Times We Had." Wall at Toompea Hill, Estonia

After following other tourists and a climb, you can see the best view of the city of Tallinn. This is 1 of 2 viewpoints, and everyone who has visited Tallinn HAS to have a picture of this wall. Not sure of the meaning behind those words, but hint of 'the times they had?' 😉

Olde Hansa

Tallinn, Estonia

Olde Hansa Medieval Restaurant in Tallinn, Estonia

Medieval Restaurant right in the center of Old Town, they cater to all tourists and can be spotted with their 15th century medieval outfits. 1221 Restorāns

Rīga, Latvia

1221 Restorāns in Rīga, Latvija

1221 is the first time this house was built in the streets of Riga's Old Town. Around this time, merchants sought autonomy from Riga's church and the rest are in books.

Three Brothers

Rīga, Latvia

Three Brothers Dwelling Houses in Rīga

The Three Brothers each a century apart is constructed by men supposedly in the same family. The one I am standing at is built in 1646 on lonely streets facing more churches. If you find images of the 'Three Brothers' online, note the small windows...this was due to property taxes that was based on window size!

Daugava River

Rīga, Latvia

Daugava River near Brīvības piemineklis in Rīga

Daugava River cannot be missed as it faces the Freedom Monument of Riga, the very place soldiers were killed during the Latvian War of Independence. This river also runs along a park and tourists can hop along Sightseeing boats for a fee.

Palace of the Grand Dukes

Vilnius, Lithuania

Entrance to the Palace of the Grand Dukes of Lithuania

Palace of the Grand Dukes of Lithuania is a center of civic education, culture and now National Museum. Located on the Cathedral Square, there is a plaque that is carved, when translated means: "a State is nothing other than a human society, whose members are bound by one God, one law, and one ruler and in which everyone looks after the common good and their own welfare".

"Freedom" painted by Millo

Vilnius, Lithuania

"Freedom" mural painted by Millo

Mural painted by artist Millo for the Vilnius Street Art Festival. Sometimes you just run into gems like this when you go out of your way to 'not be a tourist'. For those eager to find, it's near the oldest markets in the Vilnius Train district.

Hill of Three Crosses

Kalnai Park, Lithuania

Three Crosses Monument in Kalnai Park

Three Crosses Monument is a hike up the Bleak hill to get to and also a great viewpoint of Vilnius. Apparently during each restoration phase, each of the old crosses are kept inside the new ones. On a nice sunny day, you can enjoy the view of Vilnius at the front and keep cool behind in the shady area. 100% WORTH IT!

Streets of Warsaw

Warszawa, Poland

Streets of Warsaw near Kamienica Prażmowskich in Warszawa, Poland

Overlooking the Sigismund's Column you can see the diversion from the quietness of Warsaw's Old Town and towards modern Warsaw. In this very square you can find walking tours to explore both the War at Warsaw and Old Town.

Barbakan Warszawski

Warsaw, Poland

Barbakan Warszawski (Warsaw Barbican) in Warsaw, Poland

The Barbican built during the 15th century was completely destroyed during World War II and later re-constructed during the Cold War. You can easily find pictures of the Barbican online, but I wanted to highlight the land that it was built on- gave it strategic advantage over enemies during the war. Currently, it serves as a bridge between Old and New Warsaw.

Kazimierz

Kraków, Poland

Kazimierz, Old Jewish Quarter in Kraków, Poland

The most significant memory of WWII in history class is reading 'Night' written by Elie Wiesel. He was a Holocost survivor and naturally I was curious as to how these people lived during the war. I took a wonderful walking tour that highlighted the plight faced by Jews. Most of the streets have been left the same and I had the opportunity to visit the first synagoue built by Jews in the Kazimierz district.

Traces of WWII

Kraków, Poland

Traces of WWII near Gombrowiczowi – Rodacy in Kraków, Poland

Walking down the streets on the south side of the Vistula River, there were traces of WWII left. We took a look at the pharmacy of the one non-Jewish resident in the area who provided medical aid for Jews and laid account and witness over the Holocaust. Moreover, leading into the streets was the infamous Ghetto Heroes square with 70 chairs- each chair representing 1000 Jews murdered.
